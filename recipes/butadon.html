Butadon

Ingredients

For the Sauce
  • 3 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 4 tbsp boiling water
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp sake
For the Pork
  • 1/2 lb thinly sliced pork
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil
  • 2 servings cooked Japanese short-grain riche
For the Garnish
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 4 in Tokyo negi
  • Pickled red ginger

Steps

Making the Sauce
  1. Combine sugar and water in saucepan. Place on medium heat. Stir until bubbles.
  2. When mixture is amber, turn off heat. Then, using an oven mitt and a saucepan lid to protect yourself, pour the boiling water into the saucepan. After the splattering stops, turn the heat back on and mix well.
  3. Add the soy sauce and sake, bring the mixture to a simmer, then turn off the heat.
Making the Negi and Pork
  1. Cut the Tokyo negi into 4-inch pieces, then cut them lengthwise, removing the green core. Soak the outer white layers in water.
  2. Cut slits into the connective tissue between the meat and fat of the thinly sliced pork.
Cooking the Butadon
  1. Heat a frying pan over medium-high heat and add the neutral oil. Sear the pork in batches, not overcrowding the pan. Place the finished batches on a separate plate as you go.
  2. After searing all of the meat, return it all to the pan.
  3. Pour most of the sauce into the pan and coat both sides of the meat with it.
Serving
  1. Split the rice between two bowls. Drizzle some of the left over sauce over the rice. Place the meat on top.
  2. Drizzle more sauce over the meat and season with the black pepper.
  3. Garnish wih the negi and pickled red ginger.

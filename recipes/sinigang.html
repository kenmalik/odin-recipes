Sinigang

Description

Sinigang is a sour soup dish originating from the Philippines. Its main ingredient can vary, but it is common to use pork (which is what will be used in this recipe). For the souring agent, it is common to use unripe tamarind, though this can also vary.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly
  • 1 bunch spinach
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 12 pieces string beans, cut to 2 in pieces
  • 2 pieces tomato, quartered
  • 3 pieces chili
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • 2 qt water
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 pieces taro, quartered
  • 1 pack sinigang mix

Steps

  1. Heat pot and add cooking oil
  2. Sautee onion until layers separate
  3. Add pork belly and cook until lightly browned
  4. Add fish sauce and mix
  5. Pour water and bring to boil
  6. Add taro and tomatoes and simmer for 40 min or until pork is tender
  7. Add sinigang mix and chili
  8. Add string beans and simmer for 5-8 min
  9. Add spinach, turn off heat, and cover pot. Allow time for spinach to cook
  10. Serve hot
